Phase 00: Background - A Problem Worth Solving

Every great initiative is born from a pressing problem. For GigitAja, that problem is food waste. Indonesia faces a heartbreaking paradox: as one of the world's largest producers of food waste, we discard over 20 million tons of food annually.

This wasted food doesn't just disappear; it piles up in landfills, releasing methane gas. At the same time, millions of our fellow citizens still struggle with hunger.

Answering the Call of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

SDG 12: Responsible Consumption

SDG 2: Zero Hunger

SDG 13: Climate Action

Phase 02: The Blueprint - Design & Prototyping

The challenge was to craft an experience that was not only functional but also engaging for Gen-Z. I chose a vibrant, modern UI with bold typography and a casual tone.

This design adopts the principles of Clarity, Deference, and Depth. For rapid prototyping, I strategically used vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. A key moment was overcoming a browser security issue with local map tiles by implementing a solution from OpenStreetMap.

03

Phase 03: The Tangible Result

The result is a high-fidelity, fully interactive prototype that simulates the end-to-end user journey. Using `localStorage`, I single-handedly built a functional proof-of-concept demonstrating dynamic discovery, a complete simulated purchase flow, and order management.
