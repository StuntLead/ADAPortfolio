Phase 00: Background - A Problem Worth Solving

Every great initiative is born from a pressing problem. For GigitAja, that problem is food waste. Indonesia faces a heartbreaking paradox: as one of the world's largest producers of food waste, we discard over 20 million tons of food annually.

This wasted food doesn't just disappear; it piles up in landfills, releasing methane gas — a greenhouse gas dozens of times more potent than carbon dioxide and accelerating climate change. At the same time, millions of our fellow citizens still struggle with hunger.

Answering the Call of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

SDG 12: Responsible Consumption & Production

SDG 2: Zero Hunger

SDG 13: Climate Action

01

Phase 01: Project Initiation - From Idea to Action

Stemming from this urgency, the story of GigitAja began.

As a Digital Business Innovation student, my partner, Aisyah Raissa Algebra, identified a business opportunity rooted in a solution. Inspired by the success of global platforms like "Too Good To Go," she saw a significant gap in the Indonesian market. Aisyah brought the "why" (the reason to act) and the "what" (the business vision).

As a Computer Science student, I was inspired to take on the technical role — to build the "how." Our collaboration formed the perfect synergy between business innovation and technical execution. GigitAja's mission is simple: to be the bridge between delicious surplus food and hungry stomachs, saving meals from the bin in a way that is fun, easy, and affordable for our generation (Gen-Z).

02

Phase 02: The Blueprint - Design & Prototyping

The challenge was to craft an experience that was not only functional but also engaging and relatable for Gen-Z. I chose a vibrant, modern UI with bold typography and a casual tone ("Food Hero!") to make saving food feel like a cool achievement.

This design adopts the principles of Clarity, Deference, and Depth, aligning with Apple's design philosophy. For rapid prototyping, I strategically used vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. This allowed for quick iterations and testing. A key moment was overcoming a browser security issue with local map tiles by researching and implementing a more robust solution from OpenStreetMap. This pivot demonstrated my ability not just to code, but to diagnose, research, and implement reliable solutions under constraints.

03

Phase 03: The Tangible Result - Functional Prototype

The result is not just a static image; it's a high-fidelity, fully interactive prototype that simulates the end-to-end user journey. Using `localStorage`, I single-handedly built a functional proof-of-concept demonstrating dynamic discovery, a complete simulated purchase flow, and order management. This proves the concept's viability before any backend development and includes a utility "Clear History" feature for easy testing and re-demonstration.

Phase 04: The Horizon - Future Vision

This prototype is merely the launching pad. The vision for GigitAja is to evolve into a native iOS application that sets a new standard for social impact apps in Indonesia. The roadmap includes gamification, a merchant dashboard, and deep integration with Apple's ecosystem.

Native iOS Roadmap

  • Gamification & Social Sharing: Develop a badge system ("10 Meals Saved" hero) and social sharing features.
  • Merchant Dashboard: Create a simple platform for partners to upload offers and track their impact.
  • Native iOS App: Implement with SwiftUI for a beautiful interface, Apple Pay for seamless payments, and MapKit & Haptics for satisfying interactions.
